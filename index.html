What is VO2 Max?
▼

VO2 Max is the maximum rate your body can consume oxygen during intense exercise - mL/kg/min. The single best predictor of cardiovascular fitness and a strong predictor of longevity.

Why it matters for powerlifters: A higher VO2 Max means faster recovery between sets and sessions, more efficient fat burning at rest, and better adaptation to training stress.

Zone 2 (60-70% max HR) builds the aerobic base. 4x4 intervals (4 min at 90-95% max HR, 4 min easy, x4) are the single most proven protocol for VO2 Max improvement - 7-15% gains in 8-12 weeks.

Below 35 - Poor
35-42 - Below avg
42-48 - Average
48-55 - Good
55-62 - Very good
62+ - Elite

Understanding your targets
▼

TDEE is how many calories your body burns in a day including training. Calculated using the Mifflin-St Jeor formula (gender-specific) multiplied by your activity level. Set your gender in Setup for accurate results.

The deficit - to lose 0.5-1kg per week you need 500-700 kcal below TDEE. Too aggressive and you lose muscle and tank your lifts.

Protein at 1.5g/kg preserves muscle in the deficit. More important than hitting calories exactly.

Colour coding is relative to your personal calorie target. Way under means risking muscle loss. Way over means the deficit is cancelled.

What is Wilks Score?
▼

Wilks score adjusts your powerlifting total for bodyweight. A 70kg lifter and a 130kg lifter can both score 350 even though their raw totals are very different.

During a cut - your total might stay the same or dip slightly, but your Wilks score goes UP because you are moving the same weight at lower bodyweight. The best measure of progress during fat loss.

Under 200 - Beginner
200-300 - Intermediate
300-380 - Advanced
380-450 - Elite amateur
450-500 - National level
500+ - World class
